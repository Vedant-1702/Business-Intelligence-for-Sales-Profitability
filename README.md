# Business-Intelligence-for-Sales-Profitability
> An end-to-end business intelligence project that analyzes Adventure Works' sales, profitability, customer behavior, and order performance using SQL Server and Power BI. The objective is to uncover business trends, identify revenue concentration risks, evaluate profitability drivers, and translate analytical findings into actionable business recommendations.
---

##  Overview  
How is the business performing across sales, profitability, customers, and products, and what hidden risks could affect long-term growth?

Key Findings

- Generated $29.4M in total sales across the analysis period.
- Achieved $12.08M in total profit, resulting in a 41.15% profit margin.
- Processed approximately 60K customer orders from 18K unique customers.  
- Recorded an average order value of $486.09 and an average of 3.27 orders per customer.

**Why This Matters**

Although revenue remained strong, deeper analysis revealed structural risks hidden beneath headline KPIs. Revenue dependency on a small group of customers, products, and markets increases business vulnerability, while shifts in product mix directly influence profitability.

This analysis helps management move beyond descriptive reporting toward strategic decision-making.

---

##  Dataset  

- Source: Microsoft Adventure Works Data Warehouse (AdventureWorksDW) 

The project uses the Adventure Works sample database, a fictional multinational bicycle manufacturing and retail company created by Microsoft for business intelligence and analytics practice.

- Industry: Bicycle Manufacturing & Retail 
- Data Type: Sales Transactions
- Analysis Period: 2010–2014
- Total Records: ~60,000 sales transactions

## Business Recommendations
- Short-Term
  - Bundle accessories with bicycles to increase Average Order Value
  - Improve accessory profitability through pricing and bundling
  - Remove consistently low-performing SKUs
---
- Medium-Term
  - Introduce a loyalty program focused on high-value customers
  - Increase cross-selling of premium products
  - Monitor customer lifetime value
---
- Long-Term
  - Reduce dependency on United States and Australia
  - Expand into underpenetrated international markets
  - Diversify revenue across additional product categories
